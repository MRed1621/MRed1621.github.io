What is Hazing?

Definition:

As defined by the Oxford English Dictionary, hazing is the "action or an act of forcing a new or potential recruit to the military, a university fraternity or sorority, a sports team, etc., to perform strenuous, humiliating, or dangerous tasks."

The Impact of Hazing:

Hazing can happen in any organization or institution to any member. It's harmful to both individuals and organizations as a whole. To individuals, it can lead to injury, mental trauma, or even death. To organizations, it creates an unsafe and uninclusive space for members

The Extent of Hazing

Some statistics showing the extent of hazing:

-55% of students in clubs, organizations, sports, and other activities experience hazing[1]
-47% of students come to college having been hazed already[1]
-9 out of 10 students who experienced hazing do not consider themselves to have been hazed[1]
-Alcohol-related hazing, such as the encouragement of drinking games or large amounts of alcohol consumption, is one of the most frequent forms of hazing[2]
-Other hazing activities such as humiliation, sleep-deprivation, and even sexual acts are also very common forms of hazing[1]

Examples of Previous Hazing Incidents

NIU 2012

In 2012, a member of the Pi Kappa Alpha fraternity at Northern Illinois University passed away in a hazing incident. He was forced to drink extreme amounts of alcohol, and he was later found to have a blood-alcohol-level that was 5 times the legal limit for driving.[3]

Florida A&M 2011

In November 2011, a drum major in the Florida A&M University marching band passed away in another hazing incident. In this incident, he was brutally beaten on a band bus during a hazing ritual.[3] This case shows that hazing is not just prominent in Greek fraternities and sororities

Mizzou 2021

In October 2021, a Phi Gamma Delta member was hospitilized after also being forced to drink extreme amounts of alcohol. Although he survived, he was in the hospital for over 8 months, and was left blind, unable to walk, and unable to speak.[4]

Iowa 2024

In November 2024, over fifty members of Alpha Delta Phi were found in the fraternity house's basement blindfolded and covered in food.[5]
